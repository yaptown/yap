<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="Usage_notes">Usage notes</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hund&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Usage notes"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<ul><li>The normal plural is <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Hunde#German" title="Hunde">Hunde</a></i>. The plural <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/wiki/H%C3%BCnde#German" title="Hünde">Hünde</a></i> is used to some degree regionally and in colloquial varieties, chiefly in southern and western Germany, Austria and Switzerland. Compare Luxembourgish <i class="Latn mention" lang="lb"><a href="/wiki/Hond#Luxembourgish" title="Hond">Hond</a></i> (plural: <i class="Latn mention" lang="lb"><a href="/wiki/H%C3%ABnn#Luxembourgish" title="Hënn">Hënn</a></i>).</li>
<li>By analogy with the plural, the diminutive is sometimes formed without umlaut, yielding <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/wiki/Hundchen#German" title="Hundchen">Hundchen</a></i> and <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundlein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundlein (page does not exist)">Hundlein</a></i>. Regionally, <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=H%C3%BCndelein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hündelein (page does not exist)">Hündelein</a></i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundel (page does not exist)">Hundel</a></i>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hunderl&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hunderl (page does not exist)">Hunderl</a></i>, or (rarely) <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundelein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundelein (page does not exist)">Hundelein</a></i> or <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundilein&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundilein (page does not exist)">Hundilein</a></i> may be heard. The affectionate diminutive <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundi (page does not exist)">Hundi</a></i> is common throughout the whole language area.</li>
<li>To denote a male/female dog, also <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/wiki/m%C3%A4nnlich#German" title="männlich">männlicher</a>&#47;<a href="/wiki/weiblich#German" title="weiblich">weiblicher</a> Hund</i> and <i class="Latn mention" lang="de"><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundem%C3%A4nnchen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundemännchen (page does not exist)">Hundemännchen</a>&#47;<a href="/w/index.php?title=Hundeweibchen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hundeweibchen (page does not exist)">Hundeweibchen</a></i> can be used.</li></ul>
