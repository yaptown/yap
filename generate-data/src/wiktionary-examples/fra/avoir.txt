<p><i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><strong class="selflink">Avoir</strong></i> is often used with nouns like <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/chaud#French" title="chaud">chaud</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">heat</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/faim#French" title="faim">faim</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">hunger</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/soif#French" title="soif">soif</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">thirst</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/peur#French" title="peur">peur</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">fear</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span>, etc. to express a personal condition or feeling, as shown in examples above. While constructions like <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/%C3%AAtre#French" title="être">être</a> <a href="/wiki/affam%C3%A9#French" title="affamé">affamé</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">to be starving/starved</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span> and <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/%C3%AAtre#French" title="être">être</a> <a href="/wiki/assoiff%C3%A9#French" title="assoiffé">assoiffé</a></i> <span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">(</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">“</span><span class="mention-gloss">to be thirsty</span><span class="mention-gloss-double-quote">”</span><span class="mention-gloss-paren annotation-paren">)</span> exist, they are almost always used figuratively. It is always more natural to use <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><strong class="selflink">avoir</strong></i> rather than <i class="Latn mention" lang="fr"><a href="/wiki/%C3%AAtre#French" title="être">être</a></i> in the examples listed above, and other similar cases. In some cases, both verbs can be used, but with vastly different meanings:
</p>
<dl><dd><span class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="fr">J'ai chaud.</i> ― <span class="e-translation">I'm hot. (I feel hot)</span> (literally, “<span class="e-literally">I have heat.</span>”)</span></dd>
<dd><span class="h-usage-example"><i class="Latn mention e-example" lang="fr">Je suis chaud.</i> ― <span class="e-translation">I'm down for it.&#160;/&#160;I'm horny.&#160;/&#160;I'm on fire.</span> <span class="e-footer"><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">(</span><span class="ib-content qualifier-content">figuratively</span><span class="ib-brac qualifier-brac">)</span></span></span></dd></dl>
<p>Formerly, it was considered incorrect to use adverbs such as <i>très</i> in these constructions, since they would technically be modifying a noun, not an adjective. However, today, constructions such as <i>J'ai très peur</i> are considered correct and normal.
</p>
